5 Glue

Glue can be stronger than the wood itself.

  • Yellow wood glue: titebond II

Use a silicon brash or finger, credit card to spread it on the surface.

Plamping the two wood evenly.

The glue dries in 24 hours to reach its maximum strength. But you just need to wait for an hour to continue your projects.

6 Finish

6.1 Before finish

Sand it using a 220 sandpaper, remove all dust and swipe with Klean Strip.

Always finish in one direction.

6.2 Layered finish

Stack on top of wood. Provide more protection.

6.2.1 Polyurethane

  • the most popular
  • Time consuming to apply (at least 3 coat)
  • between each coast, use 320 sandpaper
  • water based poly
    • easy to apply, 3 coasts
  • oil based poly
    • usually 4 or more coasts
    • looks better
  • wipe on poly
    • can go back and force

6.2.2 Lacquer

  • dry very fast
  • brushing lacquer
    • do a little faster, and definitely not brushing back
    • no need to sand between coats (but can lightly sand it before the final coat)

6.3 Oil Finish

Into the wood. Looks more natural. Easy to apply: just wipe on the coat.

6.3.1 Linseed Oil

  • just need 2 coat

6.3.2 Tung Oil

  • 5 coats
